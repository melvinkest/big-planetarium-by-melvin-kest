Discover the wonders of our home galaxy, the Milky Way.
Explore its structure, stars, and mysteries.

Overview

Imagine the Milky Way not as something in space, but as a huge, crowded, city of light.

It resembles a huge, luminous frisbee with a huge bump in the center and long, curved fingers draping off of it. We are in half-way out of town, on one of these arms. Although we seem not to be moving, the whole system of our sun is actually riding fast in the space, with the planets moving around the center of the galaxy in the same manner as the earth about the Sun.

The galaxy is held together by Sagittarius A. It is huge, black hole, yet do not be scared of its name, it is not a vacuum cleaner in the universe that is attempting to swallow us. Rather, imagine the Sun in the centre of a solar system. Its strong gravity keeps all the stars, planets and gas clouds in their proper lanes so that the overall galaxy is spinning like a beautiful and well-organized dance.

In a nutshell, the Milky Way is a massive, rotating mass of billions of stars, and we are merely an insignificant family residing out on the suburbs, and united by some sort of magical, heavy-heartedunky cell in the center.

The Sagittarius A places our own Sun approximately 27,000 light-years out of the galactic center in the Orion Spur, it takes it almost 230 million years to orbit the galaxy once.

Although we are able to observe its bright arc in our night sky, the majority of the galaxy is a mystery, being tied together by the unknown dark matter, and being currently in a long-term collision course eventually to join our neighbor the Andromeda Galaxy.
