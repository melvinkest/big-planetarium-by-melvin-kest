A Journey Through the Milky Way

The Milky Way galaxy is a vast cosmic ocean containing at least 100 billion stars, and nearly every one of those stars is a sun to its own family of planets. While our own Solar System offers a diverse cast of characters, from scorched rocky deserts to majestic ringed giants, it is only the beginning of the story.

Beyond the reach of our Sun lies a frontier of exoplanets that defy our wildest expectations: worlds where it rains liquid glass, planets made of diamond, and rocky spheres that orbit two suns at once. By exploring these unique worlds, we don't just learn about the extreme physics of the universe; we begin to understand our own planet’s place among the stars and the tantalizing possibility that we might not be alone in the dark.
